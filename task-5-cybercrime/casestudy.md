# Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It usually occurs when attackers trick users into approving fraudulent payment requests through manipulation or misinformation.

In a typical scenario, a scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging app, pretending to be a bank representative, online seller, or customer support executive. They create a sense of urgency or trust and convince the victim that they need to approve a request in a UPI app to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment, transferring funds directly to the scammer’s account.

Students and first-time users are common targets due to lack of awareness and familiarity with digital payment systems. Fraudsters often take advantage of this by using simple but convincing techniques. The consequences of such scams include financial loss, emotional stress, and in some cases, misuse of personal or banking information.

This case study highlights the importance of understanding how digital payment systems work and being cautious while handling unknown requests. Users should never approve payment requests from unknown sources and must always verify the authenticity of any transaction before entering their UPI PIN. Awareness and careful usage are key to preventing such cybercrimes.
